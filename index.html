Who We Are

An investment partner helping to unlock more capital for transformative impact.

CAPITAL AS A FORCE is a field-based access to finance development partner focused on helping to unlock and deploy capital where it multiplies impact, deepens resilience, and creates long-term value, particularly in high-growth and high-impact markets.

We work with private and institutional capital providers, including foundations, family offices, and development partners, to design and deploy practical investment lenses, catalytic instruments, and investment frameworks that deploy capital for transformative impact.

Our approach is grounded in deep market experience and local context. We believe that capital can be an incredible force for transformation and sustainable growth, especially in Africa and other emerging markets.

The Problem We See
“Too much capital flows to what is familiar rather than what is catalytic.”

Too much capital is deployed with incomplete lenses, weak context, and poor perspective, resulting in missed opportunities, fragile outcomes, and underperforming impact, especially in emerging and frontier markets such as Africa.

As a result, capital flows to what is familiar rather than what is catalytic, and the sectors that could drive real economic transformation remain underfunded or inadequately financed.

We see a capital allocation problem, not a capital shortage. The opportunity is immense for investors willing to look deeper.

Our Core Belief

The gap is not in the availability of capital, but in the quality of perspective applied to its deployment. Better lenses produce better outcomes.

Tochukwu Ezeukwu
Africa Director

Tochukwu Ezeukwu is a finance and investment leader with over 13 years of experience mobilizing capital for high-growth enterprises and catalytic initiatives across Africa.

He most recently served as Regional Director for West Africa at the African Venture Philanthropy Alliance. Prior to this, he led the facilitation of nearly $100 million in equity and grant financing into qualifying enterprises across seven Nigerian states under the United States Agency for International Development Agribusiness Investment Activity.

Tochukwu is also the author of Capital as a Force, an investment publication written for senior investment leaders exploring how capital, when paired with perspective, can drive transformative impact.
